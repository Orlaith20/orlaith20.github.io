What Is Decimal Conversion?

Decimal conversion involves changing numbers from one form to another within the decimal number system. The decimal number system, also known as the base-10 system, is the most commonly used numeral system in the world. It consists of ten digits (0-9) and is used for everyday counting and calculations.

The three primary types of decimal conversions:

  1. Decimal to Fraction Conversion
  2. Fraction to Decimal Conversion
  3. Decimal to Percent Conversion
  4. Percent to Decimal Conversion

Decimal to Fraction Conversion

Converting decimals to fractions can be a valuable skill, especially in the context of real-world applications. To convert a decimal to a fraction:

  • Step 1: Identify the decimal number, e.g., 0.75.
  • Step 2: Write the decimal number as a fraction by placing it over a power of 10. In this case, 0.75 can be written as 75/100.
  • Step 3: Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor. For 75/100, the simplified fraction is 3/4.

Fraction to Decimal Conversion

Converting fractions to decimals is equally important. To convert a fraction to a decimal:

  • Step 1: Take the numerator and divide it by the denominator. For example, if you have 3/4, divide 3 by 4, which equals 0.75.

Decimal to Percent Conversion

Converting decimals to percentages is useful for understanding proportions and ratios. To convert a decimal to a percent:

  • Step 1: Multiply the decimal by 100. For example, to convert 0.75 to a percent, multiply it by 100 to get 75%.

Percent to Decimal Conversion

Converting percentages to decimals is necessary for various mathematical calculations. To convert a percent to a decimal:

  • Step 1: Divide the percentage by 100. For example, to convert 75% to a decimal, divide 75 by 100 to get 0.75.
